Deep Matters: Foundations

A one-day academic conference on foundation models in materials science

6 NOVEMBER 2025 · LONDON

Deep Matters: Foundations is the first edition of the Deep Matters international conference series, dedicated to exploring how foundation models are transforming materials research and innovation.

This year’s conference features speakers from NVIDIA, Cusp AI, Orbital Materials, Polaron, and leading academic institutions. Bringing together academia, established companies, and startups, the event offers a unique forum where scientific advances meet industrial applications and entrepreneurial innovation.

Code of Conduct

We are committed to providing a respectful, inclusive, and safe environment for all participants.

  • Treat everyone with respect and professionalism.
  • Harassment, discrimination, or disruptive behavior will not be tolerated.
  • Discussions should remain constructive and focused on science and innovation.
  • Report any concerns to the organizing committee immediately.

All attendees, speakers, and organizers are expected to abide by this Code of Conduct throughout the conference.

Frequently Asked Questions

When and where is the conference?

The conference will take place on 6 November 2025 at Google Cloud Startup Hub, 4–5 Bonhill Street, London EC2A 4BX, UK.

Is registration free?

Yes — the event is completely free, but seats are limited (~100) and allocated on a first-come, first-served basis.

When does registration close?

Registration will remain open until all seats are filled. We recommend registering early.

What is the schedule?

It is a one-day program, running from morning to evening.

Who are the speakers?

Speakers include leading voices from academia, industry, and startups working at the intersection of AI and materials science.

Is food provided?

Yes — coffee breaks and lunch will be provided.

How do I get there?

The venue is within walking distance of Old Street (5 min), Moorgate (7–10 min), and Liverpool Street (8–10 min) stations, with bus routes 21, 35, 135, 141, and 205 nearby.

Will the talks be recorded or live-streamed?

The conference will be in-person only. No live-stream or recording is planned.

What if I can't attend after registering?

Please email us at g.lei23@imperial.ac.uk if you can no longer attend, so we may reallocate your seat.

Is the venue accessible?

Yes — the venue has step-free access and accessible facilities. Please contact us in advance if you have specific needs.
